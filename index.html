Start Your One Book, One Month Journey

Building a reading habit doesn’t have to be complicated. With a simple plan, a single book, and a few minutes each day, you can finally finish the books you’ve always wanted to read.

The 3-Step Method

  1. Choose One Book

    Pick a single, manageable book for the month. Shorter, engaging reads work best when you’re building a new habit.

  2. Read a Little Each Day

    Commit to a small daily goal—like 10 pages or 15 minutes. Consistency matters more than intensity.

  3. Track Your Progress

    Use the monthly tracker to mark each day you read. Watching your streak grow keeps you motivated.

Beginner-Friendly Book Ideas

Not sure where to start? These approachable books are great for busy readers and beginners.

“Atomic Habits” by James Clear

A practical guide to building better habits through tiny, consistent changes. Short chapters make it easy to read in small daily chunks.

“The Alchemist” by Paulo Coelho

A short, inspiring novel about following your dreams. Simple language and a fast pace make it a great choice for new or returning readers.

“The Little Book of Hygge” by Meik Wiking

A cozy, easy-to-read book about finding comfort in everyday moments. Its brief sections work perfectly for quick daily reading.

“Deep Work” by Cal Newport

A focused look at concentration in a distracted world. Ideal for readers who want motivation to protect their time and attention.
